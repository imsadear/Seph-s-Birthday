Why Philippine speech technology needs open resources

🌐

Many languages are underrepresented

Of the Philippines' 130+ living languages, only a handful have meaningful digital presence. Tagalog dominates while regional languages like Waray, Tausug, and Kapampangan remain invisible to AI and speech systems.

💾

Developers need clean datasets

Researchers and engineers building speech recognition or translation tools struggle to find structured, labeled, and high-quality data for Philippine languages. What exists is often scattered, inconsistent, or locked behind paywalls.

🤝

Communities should lead documentation

Language knowledge belongs to the people who speak it. Top-down academic documentation often misses nuance, dialect variation, and everyday usage. Wikapedia puts the community at the center of the process.

From community contribution to developer-ready resources

  1. 01
    ✍️

    Contribute

    Filipino speakers add words, phrases, translations, audio recordings, and pronunciation notes in their native language directly through the platform.

  2. 02
    🤖

    AI Check

    Submitted entries pass through AI-powered checks for duplicates, language detection, translation confidence, audio clarity, and safety — flagging issues for human review.

  3. 03
    👥

    Community Review

    Native speakers and trusted community reviewers validate entries for accuracy, cultural context, and regional nuance. Human judgment always takes precedence.

  4. 04
    📦

    Publish

    Verified entries are published as structured open-source datasets — downloadable language packs, APIs, and benchmark sets ready for developers building Filipino language technology.

Bringing every Filipino voice into the light

The Problem

The Philippines has over 130 living languages, yet only Tagalog and English are meaningfully represented in AI and speech technology. Millions of Filipinos who speak Waray, Tausug, Kapampangan, Ilocano, and dozens of other languages are effectively invisible to the digital tools that are reshaping how people communicate, access healthcare, and participate in society.

The Solution

Wikapedia is a community-powered, AI-assisted platform that lets speakers of any Philippine language contribute verified language data. By combining community knowledge with AI quality checks and human review, we create high-quality, structured language resources that any developer can use freely.

The Impact

Every verified entry becomes a building block for the future of Filipino language technology. Better speech recognition for emergency services. More inclusive voice assistants. Translation tools that work in Bisaya. Text-to-speech engines that sound like home. Our goal is to make every Filipino language accessible, searchable, and usable by developers building the next generation of tools.

Our Ethical Principles

  • 🏠
    Community Ownership

    Language data belongs to the communities that created it. Contributors retain recognition and communities retain voice over how their language is documented.

  • 🔓
    Open Access

    All verified language resources are freely available under open licenses. No paywalls, no corporate lock-in. Built for the public good.

  • ✍️
    Ethical Consent

    Every contributor explicitly consents to how their data is used. Recordings and personal contributions are never used beyond the stated purpose without renewed consent.

  • 👤
    Human-centered AI

    AI is a tool, not a gatekeeper. Native speakers and community reviewers always have final say over what is verified and what is published.

  • 🌺
    Respect for Regional Variation

    We document dialects, regional usage, and variation — not just a single "standard" form. Language is living and regional differences are valued, not erased.

  • 🛠️
    Developer Usability

    We structure data so it is immediately useful to developers — with clear schemas, status labels, and export formats designed for real-world AI pipelines.
